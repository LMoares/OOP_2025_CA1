Asbestos-Containing Materials
- Handling:
- Never cut, break, or sand asbestos materials 
  — fibers are highly dangerous if airborne.
- Keep items intact and damp if possible to reduce dust.
- Store securely in sealed, labeled bags or containers until disposal.
- Where to Dispose:
- Do not place in household bins or recycling.
- Must be taken to specialized hazardous waste facilities or civic amenity 
  sites that accept asbestos.
- Licensed contractors may be required for removal and disposal.
- PPE Required:
- Disposable coveralls, gloves, goggles, and a certified respirator (P2 or P3).
- Never handle asbestos without proper protective gear.
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
⚗️ Mixed Chemical Sludge
- Handling:
- Treat as hazardous by default — composition is unknown and may include 
  toxic, corrosive, or flammable substances.
- Store in sealed, labeled containers resistant to chemical corrosion.
- Never pour into drains, soil, or general waste.
- Where to Dispose:
- Bring to Household Hazardous Waste (HHW) centres or civic amenity sites.
- For industrial quantities, contact specialized hazardous waste contractors.
- PPE Required:
- Chemical-resistant gloves, goggles, respirator, and protective clothing.
- Handle only in well-ventilated areas or outdoors.
