Old Fuel (Petrol or Diesel)
- Handling:
- Store in sealed, approved fuel containers.
- Keep away from heat, sparks, or open flames.
- Never pour onto soil, drains, or household bins.
- Where to Dispose:
- Bring to Household Hazardous Waste (HHW) centres or civic amenity sites.
- Some garages or recycling centres accept old fuel 
  for safe disposal or reprocessing.
- PPE Required:
- Chemical-resistant gloves, goggles, and respirator for fumes.
- Protective clothing to avoid skin contact.

- Handling:
- Store in tightly sealed containers, away from heat sources.
- Do not mix with other chemicals.
- Small amounts of pure acetone may evaporate safely, 
  but contaminated remover must be treated as hazardous.
- Where to Dispose:
- HHW collection centres for contaminated remover.
- Empty, rinsed bottles may be recyclable depending on local rules.
- PPE Required:
- Gloves, goggles, and mask/respirator if handling 
  large volumes or contaminated remover.

- Handling:
- Store upright, sealed, and away from heat or flames (highly flammable).
- Do not pour into drains or mix with other cleaners.
- Where to Dispose:
- HHW centres for large volumes or expired products.
- Small amounts can be absorbed into cat litter or paper towels, 
  sealed in bags, and placed in general waste.
- Empty glass bottles may be recyclable once rinsed.
- PPE Required:
- Gloves and goggles; respirator if handling large volumes due to fumes.
