Aerosol Cans with Toxic Contents
- Handling:
- Do not puncture, crush, or incinerate — pressurized cans can explode.
- Store upright in a cool, ventilated area away from heat or flames.
- Keep in original containers with labels intact.
- Where to Dispose:
- Bring to Household Hazardous Waste (HHW) centres or civic amenity sites.
- Empty, non-toxic aerosol cans (e.g., deodorant, air freshener) 
  may be recyclable as metals, 
  but toxic contents (paints, pesticides, cleaners) must go to HHW.
- PPE Required:
- Gloves, goggles, and mask/respirator to avoid inhaling fumes.
- Protective clothing if handling multiple cans or leaks.
-------------------------------------------------------------------------------
⚗️ Unknown Chemical Mixtures
- Handling:
- Treat as hazardous by default — never mix further or pour into drains.
- Store in sealed, labeled containers until identified.
- Avoid skin contact and inhalation.
- Where to Dispose:
- HHW centres are the safest option for unidentified chemicals.
- Do not attempt home disposal — unknown reactions may occur.
- PPE Required:
- Gloves, goggles, respirator, and protective clothing.
- Handle in well-ventilated areas only.
