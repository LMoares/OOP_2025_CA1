Hazardous Waste:
1. Bring to your local amenity site or
2. garages/Motor Factors 
(If they replace your battery, they are legally obliged to take the old one). 
3. Metal Scrap Recyclers (Like O'Reilly Recycling).
Car Batteries:
DO NOT PUT IN HOUSEHOLD BIN!
Do NOT drain the acid. Handle carefully and transport upright.
Toxicity:
HIGH. They contain corrosive Sulfuric Acid (electrolyte) and toxic Lead.
PPE:
Safety Goggles (splash risk) and Acid-Resistant Gloves 
(rubber or thick nitrile) are highly recommended.
Damaged/Leaking Battery?
Full Face Shield (or safety goggles) and Acid-Resistant Gloves. 
Long sleeves/pants or an apron are best.
##########################################################################
Rechargeable Batteries:
DO NOT PUT IN HOUSEHOLD BINS!
Action:
Tape the terminals (the positive and negative ends) of Li-ion batteries 
with clear sticky tape to prevent short-circuiting and fires.
1. Bring to your local amenity site (for larger batteries/quantities). 
2. Retailers (Shops, supermarkets, and electrical retailers that sell these 
   types of batteries must accept them back for free). 
3. Blue Battery Boxes (Widely available in local shops and supermarkets).
TOXICITY:
HIGH. They contain toxic metals: 
Cadmium (in Ni-Cd) and Cobalt/Nickel (in Li-ion). 
Li-ion also poses a fire hazard if terminals short.
PPE:
Disposable Gloves (nitrile or latex) 
are recommended for general handling and residue.
Damaged/Swollen/Hot Battery?
Disposable Gloves and safety glasses. Approach with caution.
